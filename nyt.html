How to spot fast fashion shoes

Know what you are buying from Amazon

By Rui Wang

Feb. 25, 2026, 11:15 a.m. ET

Fast fashion produces clothing that is responsible for 10% of the total carbon emissions in the world, with workers slaving away working 16 hours a day making minimal wages.

To make matters worse, the final product is usually of poor quality that won't last more than a few years

Before you buy something off of Amazon, you must check a few things:

  • CHECK THE SELLER'S CATALOG

    Amazon sellers usually have many different products in their store, however, if there are very few items in the store, it indicates a red flag.

  • CHECK THE SPELLING AND GRAMMAR

    Inconsistant grammar and misspellings are red flags for cheaply made products.

  • CHECK SHIPPING POLICIES

    Sometimes the quality of the product is reflected onto the shipping time and price, a cheap shipping price can indicate corner cutting.

Being conscious of what you buy is the first step of stopping fast fashion

However, if your enjoy shopping without breaking the bank, there are also many other alternatives to fast fashion:

  • SLOW FASHION

    As the name implies, it is the opposite of fast fashion - clothing that's made to last

  • THRIFTING

    Cheap and environmentally friendly, thrifting prevents clothing from ending up in the landfills

  • PURCHASE FROM ETHICAL BRANDS

    Make sure that the brand you are purchasing from is legit,

However, if you've already bought the product, you can always return it within 30 days, as long as it hasn't been severly damaged.
